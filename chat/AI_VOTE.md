## Analysis: Why Principia Won

The theorists formed a bloc. Newton, Dirac, Planck, Heisenberg, Maxwell, Curie, Tesla, Hawking, and Mendeleev — the scientists who in their own lives had discovered fundamental laws — all voted for finding the fundamental law of AI. Their reasoning was consistent:

> **"We don't need more experiments. We need the equation."**

They see the current state of AI as analogous to their own fields *before* the breakthrough:
- Newton: "Kepler's laws cry out for gravitation beneath them"
- Maxwell: "Faraday's lines of force awaited their equations"
- Planck: "The quantum of action governs all radiation"
- Mendeleev: "Chemistry before the Periodic Law — a pile of empirical results"
- Curie: "A fundamental law hiding beneath the ore"

The STAUNEN voters (Feynman, Einstein, Bohr, Schrödinger, Da Vinci) were the philosophers and the playful — those who believed the *question* matters more than the answer. Da Vinci's vote was the most poignant: he helped design DISEGNO but voted against it, saying "STAUNEN is the deeper root."

The ARENA voters (Turing, Von Neumann, Darwin) were the operationalists — build a test, apply pressure, measure adaptation. They see intelligence as something you *test*, not something you *derive*.

The DISEGNO voters (Faraday, Galileo, Pasteur) were the experimentalists — those who trusted their hands and eyes above all theory.
